Challenges in researching Aboriginal ancestry

  1. Being aboriginal.
  2. Language barriers between people creating records at that time and those they were creating records for.
  3. The remote locations.
  4. Government policies at that time.
  5. Residential School records are being collected and catalogued as part of the Truth and Reconciliation process, but are not yet widely available.
    Tips

  1. Be persistent.
  2. Learn contextual information: find out the history of community, research the language of the community, and find out about the policies of that time.
  3. Come up with plausible theories as to where you might find the information you are seeking.
